# Retention Intelligence Engine

### Customer Churn Analytics & Revenue Retention Strategy

> End-to-end data analytics project focused on identifying churn drivers, customer behavior patterns, and designing business strategies to improve long-term retention and recurring revenue.

---

## Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the most critical business challenges for subscription-based companies. High churn directly impacts recurring revenue, customer lifetime value, and long-term profitability.

This project performs a comprehensive data analysis to identify the key factors influencing customer churn and uncover actionable strategies that businesses can implement to improve customer retention.

Rather than simply identifying whether customers leave, the objective is to understand **why customers leave** and translate data insights into measurable business decisions.

---

## Business Problem

Companies spend significant resources acquiring customers, yet many lose customers shortly after acquisition.

The central business challenge:

* Which customers are most likely to churn?
* What behavioral patterns drive customer cancellation?
* Which business interventions reduce churn probability?
* How can companies improve long-term customer retention?

This project simulates a real-world business analytics workflow used by modern data teams.

## Key Insights

### 1. Contract Type Is The Strongest Churn Driver

Customers subscribed under **month-to-month contracts** demonstrate significantly higher churn compared to long-term contract customers.

**Business Impact**

Low commitment enables easy switching to competitors.

---

### 2. Customer Churn Concentrates In Early Lifecycle

A large percentage of churn occurs during the first few months after customer acquisition.

**Business Impact**

Customer onboarding experience during the first 90 days is critical for retention.

---

### 3. Value Added Services Improve Retention

Customers subscribed to:

* Online Security
* Tech Support
* Device Protection

show consistently lower churn rates.

**Business Impact**

Additional services increase product dependency and switching cost.

---

### 4. Payment Behavior Predicts Churn Risk

Customers using **Electronic Check payments** demonstrate elevated churn behavior.

**Business Impact**

This customer segment may represent price-sensitive or low-engagement users.

---

### 5. Customer Behavior Outperforms Demographics

Demographic variables such as gender show minimal correlation with churn outcomes.

**Business Impact**

Behavioral signals are significantly stronger predictors than demographic segmentation.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

Based on analysis findings, businesses should prioritize:

* Strengthening onboarding during the first 90 days
* Converting month-to-month users into long-term contracts
* Bundling Online Security and Tech Support into subscription packages
* Building retention campaigns targeting high-risk customers
* Implementing predictive churn monitoring systems
